About us

OUR MISSION

As Vanderbilt’s Asian American Student Association (AASA), our purpose is to promote and provide a cultural and political awareness of Asian and Asian-American customs and diversity within the University and local community.

Meet our president

Welcome to Vandy, Class of 2020!

Three years ago, I was in your shoes - bright-eyed, curious...and slightly confused. Trying to find my way around campus, living on my own, and hoping to at least pass my classes, I took any opportunity to find a way to balance my school work and the stress of not knowing. That's when I went to the student involvement fair and came across 83736e16 organizations - among them, AASA.

AASA aims to promote cultural awareness and education about Asian and Asian-American culture to not only the Vanderbilt community, but also the surrounding Nashville community. We fulfill this mission in so many different ways, whether that is through thought-provoking panel discussions, teaching and learning traditional dances, or sharing our love of delicious Asian food. However, our actions go beyond our mission. As one of the largest multicultural orgs on campus, AASA strives to build a community - a family away from home. We welcome people from any demography because diversity in experiences strengthens our community and facilitates the fulfillment of our mission.

In the wise words of Drake, "We got a really big team, and we [do some really big things]." Our board members have created and continuously find more opportunities for the Vandy community to get involved with AASA. A few of our major events are highlighted in this newsletter, including our signature kick-off event, the Freshman and Transfer Mixer, but to fully capture all that we do throughout the year can only be done through experience. We invite you to share your years at Vanderbilt with us and encourage you to reach out to any AASA member for anything ranging from questions to casual conversation. We are so happy you are here and cannot wait for our AASA family to keep growing.

Anchor Down,
Amy Dam
